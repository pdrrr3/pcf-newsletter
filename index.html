Part 1 — Psychological Profile

What the GTA precon realtor
is feeling right now.

The dominant emotions, ranked by intensity. Understanding these is the foundation of any content strategy that actually connects.

#1 DOMINANT EMOTION

Exhaustion

"How much longer can I hold on?"

2+ years of declining market with no confirmed bottom. The mental toll of commission-based income unpredictability is severe — real estate has the second-highest rate of clinically diagnosed depression among all professions.

#2

Financial Anxiety

"Am I going to make it?"

Income at generational lows while fixed costs climb. ORWP fees surged 700%, RECO insurance, board fees, brokerage costs — all due whether you close zero deals or twenty. TRREB membership dropped 8%.

#3

Distrust

"I've heard 'recovery' before."

Deep skepticism of optimistic forecasts, developer promises, and government announcements. They've watched 28 projects cancel. They need proof, not predictions.

#4

Isolation

"Everyone's leaving."

Fewer peers (agents leaving the industry), fewer deals, fewer VIP events, less community. The social fabric of the precon world has thinned dramatically.

#5

Hunger for Direction

"Tell me what to DO."

Desperate for someone who isn't just reporting what's happening, but telling them what concrete action to take this week. Theory is useless. Scripts, plays, and tactics are gold.

#6

Guarded Optimism

"There are some signals..."

Rates at 2.25%. HST rebate worth $130K. Foreign buyer ban expiring Jan 2027. Supply correction underway. The signals are real — but they've been burned before.

Personality Frameworks — Who We're Talking To

DISC PROFILE

High D / High I

The Driven Connector

Precon realtors skew heavily Dominant (results-oriented, competitive, direct) and Influential (relationship-driven, persuasive, optimistic). In a downturn, High-D types become impatient and aggressive — they want action, not analysis. High-I types lose their energy source (social proof, events, deal flow).

Content implication: Lead with data and directives, not feelings. Give them plays to run, not empathy essays.

MASLOW'S HIERARCHY

Dropped to Safety

From self-actualization to survival

In the boom, precon agents operated at Esteem/Self-Actualization — status, recognition, luxury. The downturn has dropped them to Safety (financial security, job stability) and even Belonging (community erosion as peers leave). Content must meet them where they are now, not where they were.

Content implication: Address safety first (income opportunities, cost management), then rebuild belonging (community, shared identity).

STAGES OF GRIEF

Between Anger & Bargaining

The market they built their career on is gone

Many precon agents are grieving a lost identity. The stages map clearly: Denial (2023 — "it'll bounce back"), Anger (2024 — "developers are failing us"), Bargaining (now — "if I pivot to resale..."), with Depression and Acceptance still ahead for some.

Content implication: Don't skip ahead to Acceptance. Meet them in Bargaining — give them concrete pivots and alternatives that validate their instinct to adapt.

JOBS TO BE DONE

Four Core Jobs

What they're hiring content to do

1. Find deals — "Help me find something to sell"
2. Manage risk — "Help me avoid selling a project that cancels"
3. Stay relevant — "Help me prove my value to clients"
4. Plan an exit (or a pivot) — "Help me figure out what's next"

Content implication: Every issue should serve at least 2 of these 4 jobs. Launch Radar = Job 1. Developer vetting = Job 2. Policy Decoder = Job 3. The Play = Job 4.

05

THE BOTTOM LINE

One forward-looking indicator with an honest assessment. No cheerleading. Agents trust you because you don't sugarcoat.

From: The Precon Brief <hello@preconbrief.com>
To: you
Subject: $1,500 per home. That's what tariffs cost your buyers now.

THE NUMBER: $1,500+

That's the estimated additional cost per home from US tariffs on steel, aluminum, and lumber that took effect in 2025.

Steel and aluminum face 25% tariffs. Lumber got hit with 45% combined duties. And roughly 40% of HVAC/mechanical equipment used in GTA high-rises is imported from the US.

Your buyers are going to ask about this. Here's exactly what to tell them.

POLICY DECODER: The tariff situation in plain English

In Feb 2025, Trump imposed 25% tariffs on most Canadian imports. Canada retaliated. Then 25% on steel/aluminum (March 2025), then autos (April 2025).

The Supreme Court struck down some tariffs in Feb 2026, but steel, aluminum, and lumber tariffs were NOT affected — those stay.

What this means for precon: construction costs go up modestly ($1,500-$3,000 per unit). But the bigger impact is confidence. The trade war creates uncertainty, and uncertainty kills buyer decisions.

The 30-second buyer script:

"Yes, tariffs are adding some cost to new construction — roughly $1,500-$3,000 per unit. But here's the thing: that cost increase is locked in by the developer at today's prices. If you wait, and tariffs go higher, the next round of pricing will be worse. Buying now actually protects you from further increases."

THE PLAY: Use tariffs as urgency, not fear

Most agents either ignore tariffs or use them to create panic. Both are wrong. The smart play is to position them as a reason to lock in pricing now.

Send this to 5 fence-sitting buyers this week:

"Hi [name], quick note — you may have seen coverage about US tariffs on construction materials. The impact on current precon pricing is modest ($1,500-$3,000/unit), but developers launching later this year will price those costs in from the start. The projects available now were priced before the full tariff impact. Something to consider as you're evaluating timing."

THE BOTTOM LINE

Most developers pricing projects in Q1-Q2 2026 are eating the tariff increase rather than passing it on, because demand is too fragile. But that math doesn't last forever.

Projects launching in late 2026 and 2027 will have tariff costs baked into pricing from day one.

Current pricing is as tariff-insulated as it's going to get.
